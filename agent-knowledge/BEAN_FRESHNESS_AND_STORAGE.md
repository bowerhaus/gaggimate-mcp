# Bean Freshness & Storage

Quick reference for freshness windows, ultra-fresh handling, and visual cues during dialing-in.

*Adapted from [gaggimate-barista](https://github.com/charleshall888/gaggimate-barista) by Charlie Hall.*

---

## CO2 Release Timeline

| Period | CO2 Released | What's Happening |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| During roasting | ~50% of total | Gas escapes as the bean cracks and expands |
| First 24 hours | ~40% of remaining | Rapid off-gassing from the porous structure |
| Days 1–7 | Exponential decay | Rate halves roughly every 1–2 days |
| Days 7–14 | Slow release | Most gas gone; flavors stabilize |
| Days 14–30 | Trace amounts | Bean has reached equilibrium |

## Peak Flavor Windows

### By Roast Level

| Roast Level | Minimum Rest | Peak Window | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Light | 10–14 days | 14–28 days | Dense structure needs time; complexity develops late |
| Medium | 7–10 days | 10–21 days | The classic sweet spot; most forgiving |
| Medium-Dark | 5–7 days | 7–14 days | Opens up quickly; use within 3 weeks |
| Dark | 3–5 days | 5–14 days | Ready fast, stales fast; best very fresh |

### Processing Method Influence

Natural and anaerobic coffees often benefit from *longer* rest periods than washed coffees at the same roast level. The residual fruit compounds and fermentation-derived volatiles need time to integrate:

- **Washed:** Peak at the earlier end of the roast-level window
- **Natural:** Add 2–3 days to the minimum rest
- **Anaerobic/Experimental:** Add 3–5 days; these coffees can taste chaotic when too fresh

## Ultra-Fresh Handling (1–7 Days Off Roast)

### What Happens When You Brew Too Fresh

- **CO2 disruption:** Gas pockets in the puck create voids, interrupting water-coffee contact → uneven extraction
- **Channeling risk:** Gas bubbles create preferential flow paths → simultaneous over- and under-extraction
- **Flavor:** Sour, sharp, underdeveloped. Sweetness is masked. Aggressive, unstable crema
- **Puck behavior:** Excessive swelling during pre-infusion, soupy spent puck

### Profile Adjustments for Fresh Beans

| Adjustment | Why | How |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Extended bloom (10–15 seconds) | Allows CO2 to escape before extraction pressure | Use a bloom profile with pump-off pause |
| Slower pre-infusion flow | Gentle saturation prevents gas-driven channeling | 1.5–2 ml/s instead of 2–3 ml/s |
| Slightly coarser grind | CO2 adds resistance; compensate to hit target time | 1–2 micro steps coarser than rested baseline |
| Higher temperature (+1°C) | Fresh beans resist extraction; heat compensates | Don't exceed roast-level ceiling |

### Can Fresh Beans Work?

Dark roasts can be brewed as early as day 3 with decent results — they degas quickly and extract readily. Light roasts at 3 days are an uphill battle. If you must brew light roasts ultra-fresh, a turbo-style profile (coarse grind, low pressure, high ratio) is more forgiving than traditional espresso.

## Visual Freshness Cues

### Crema Diagnosis

| Crema Appearance | Freshness Indicator |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Thick, pale, bubbly, dissipates quickly | Too fresh (1–5 days) — excess CO2 |
| Golden-brown, fine texture, lingers 2+ minutes | Sweet spot (7–21 days) |
| Thin, pale tan, disappears in seconds | Past peak (30+ days) |
| Almost no crema | Stale (45+ days) or very coarse grind |

### Puck Behavior

- **Fresh (1–7 days):** Puck swells noticeably during pre-infusion as trapped CO2 expands. Spent puck may be soupy or have a bubbly surface.
- **Rested (7–21 days):** Moderate swelling. Spent puck is firm and cohesive — the "hockey puck" you want to see.
- **Stale (30+ days):** Minimal swelling. Spent puck may be dry and crumbly, or muddy if it channeled.

### Bloom Behavior (During Bloom Phase)

On machines with a bloom phase, watch the pressure gauge after the pump pauses:

- **Fresh beans:** Pressure may *rise* briefly as CO2 escapes into the headspace
- **Rested beans:** Pressure stays stable or drops slowly
- **Stale beans:** Pressure drops immediately — no gas to sustain it
