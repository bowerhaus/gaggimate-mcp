# Espresso Filter Baskets

Quick reference for how your basket affects extraction during dialing-in.

*Adapted from [gaggimate-barista](https://github.com/charleshall888/gaggimate-barista) by Charlie Hall.*

---

## What "22g" Actually Means

The basket size (15g, 18g, 20g, 22g) indicates the **nominal dose capacity** — the amount of ground coffee the basket is designed to hold while maintaining proper headroom between the puck and the shower screen.

- **Dose = basket size.** A 22g basket should be dosed with 22g of coffee. Don't underdose.
- **Tolerance range:** VST specifies ±1g (21-23g for a 22g basket). IMS has a slightly wider range.
- **Underdosing risks:** Too much headroom between puck and shower screen → water pools above the puck → uneven saturation → channeling.
- **Overdosing risks:** Puck touches the shower screen → imprint on the puck ("screen kiss") → restricted flow → uneven extraction and potential back-pressure.

**How to check headroom:** After pulling a shot, look at the top of the puck. It should be flat and smooth, with no screen imprint. If you see a mesh pattern pressed into the surface, reduce your dose by 0.5g.

---

## Depth and Diameter

All standard 58mm baskets share the same diameter. The difference between sizes is **depth** — a 22g basket (28mm deep on IMS) holds more coffee than an 18g (24mm) because the puck is taller, not wider.

A deeper puck has different extraction characteristics:

- **More uniform flow** — water must travel through more coffee, creating more even resistance across the bed
- **Less channeling** — thicker pucks are harder for water to shortcut through
- **More resistance** — deeper puck = longer contact time at the same grind setting, so you may need to grind slightly coarser
- **Optimal depth-to-width ratio** is approximately 2:3 for balanced resistance and even water distribution

## Precision Baskets & Puck Prep

Precision baskets are more uniform, but they're also less forgiving. The uniform hole pattern means water flows evenly *if* the puck is evenly distributed — but it will also faithfully reveal any distribution flaws.

**With a stock basket:** Random hole sizes mean water flow is already uneven. Your distribution doesn't matter as much because the basket is the bigger variable.

**With a precision basket:** The basket is no longer the weak link. Now your puck prep becomes the limiting factor. This means:

- **WDT is more important** — clumps that a stock basket might mask will create visible channeling with a precision basket
- **Even tamping matters more** — a tilted tamp creates a thinner section that channels first
- **Dose consistency matters** — because precision baskets have tighter dose windows

This is a feature, not a bug. A precision basket raises the extraction ceiling, but you have to do your part with puck prep to reach it. The combination of precision basket + good WDT + even tamp is what produces genuinely even extractions.
