# Coffee Research Checklist

Detailed procedures for researching coffee beans.

*Adapted from [gaggimate-barista](https://github.com/charleshall888/gaggimate-barista) by Charlie Hall.*

---

## Web Search Strategy

### Primary Sources (in order of reliability)
1. **Roaster's website** — Most accurate for their own coffee
2. **Specialty coffee databases** — Trade Coffee, Bean Box listings
3. **Coffee review sites** — detailed tasting notes and roast analysis

### Search Queries
- `"[roaster name]" "[coffee name]"` — exact match
- `"[roaster name]" [origin] [process]` — if name unclear
- `[origin] [variety] espresso` — for general origin guidance

## Inferring Roast Level

When roast level isn't stated, infer from tasting notes:

### Light Roast Indicators
- Fruit-forward: blueberry, strawberry, citrus, stone fruit
- Floral: jasmine, lavender, bergamot
- Tea-like descriptors
- "Bright," "vibrant," "complex acidity"

### Medium Roast Indicators
- Balanced fruit + chocolate
- Nutty: almond, hazelnut
- Caramel, brown sugar
- "Balanced," "smooth," "approachable"

### Dark Roast Indicators
- Chocolate dominant: dark chocolate, cocoa
- Roasty: toasted, smoky
- Low acidity descriptors
- "Bold," "full-bodied," "rich"

## Origin Extraction Patterns

### Brazilian
- **Character:** Nutty, chocolate, low acidity, full body
- **Processing:** Natural or pulped natural common
- **Approach:** Lower temps (90-93°C), classic 9-bar or gentle profiles
- **Ratio:** 1:2 or shorter for intensity

### Central American (Guatemala, Honduras)
- **Character:** Balanced, chocolate, mild fruit, clean sweetness
- **Processing:** Mostly washed
- **Approach:** Classic profiles, 92-94°C, 9 bar
- **Ratio:** 1:2 standard

### Colombian
- **Character:** Balanced, sweet, mild acidity, caramel
- **Processing:** Mostly washed; growing experimental/anaerobic scene
- **Approach:** Classic 9-bar works well, 92-94°C
- **Ratio:** 1:2 standard

### Costa Rican
- **Character:** Clean sweetness, stone fruit, honey-forward when honey processed
- **Processing:** Known for honey process innovation (yellow → red → black, increasing sweetness and body)
- **Approach:** Washed: 92-94°C, classic 9-bar. Honey: 92-94°C, 8-9 bar, bloom profile for black honey
- **Ratio:** 1:2 for washed; 1:2 to 1:2.5 for honey

### Ethiopian
- **Character:** Fruit-forward, floral, complex
- **Processing:** Often natural (fruit bomb) or washed (floral, tea-like)
- **Approach:** Higher temps (94-96°C), bloom profiles for naturals
- **Ratio:** 1:2.5 for fruit development

### Indian
- **Character:** Earthy, spicy, heavy body (Monsooned Malabar); emerging specialty scene
- **Processing:** Monsooned (unique — beans exposed to monsoon winds); washed and natural for specialty
- **Approach:** Monsooned: lower temps (89-91°C), 7-8 bar. Specialty washed: 92-94°C standard
- **Ratio:** Monsooned: 1:1.5 to 1:2. Specialty: 1:2 standard

### Indonesian (Sumatra, Java)
- **Character:** Earthy, herbal, full body, cedar, tobacco
- **Processing:** Wet-hulled (Giling Basah)
- **Approach:** Lower temps (90-92°C), shorter ratios, 8-9 bar
- **Ratio:** 1:1.5 to 1:2 for body

### Kenyan
- **Character:** Bright, berry, blackcurrant, wine-like acidity, complex
- **Processing:** Washed (double fermentation)
- **Approach:** High temps (94-96°C) to extract sweetness behind the acidity
- **Ratio:** 1:2.5 to 1:3 for clarity

### Panamanian (Gesha Focus)
- **Character:** Jasmine, bergamot, tropical fruit, tea-like body
- **Processing:** Washed, natural, honey
- **Approach:** Gentle: 93-95°C, 7-8 bar, bloom profile. Avoid high pressure — Gesha is fragile
- **Ratio:** 1:2.5 to 1:3 for clarity and aromatics

### Rwandan / Burundian
- **Character:** Bright, berry, citrus, floral — distinct from Ethiopian and Kenyan
- **Processing:** Washed
- **Approach:** 93-95°C, 8-9 bar, bloom profile enhances fruit development
- **Ratio:** 1:2.5 for clarity

### Yemeni
- **Character:** Wild, fruity, wine-like, dried fruit, spice, chocolate
- **Processing:** Natural (traditional dry process)
- **Approach:** 93-95°C, 7-8 bar, bloom profile
- **Ratio:** 1:2 to 1:2.5

## Variety Extraction Patterns

### Altitude and Density

| Altitude (masl) | Density | Grind Approach | Temp Approach |
|-----------------|---------|---------------|---------------|
| < 1200 | Low | Standard or slightly coarser | Per roast level |
| 1200–1600 | Medium | Standard baseline | Per roast level |
| 1600–2000 | High | Standard or slightly finer | +1°C from roast baseline |
| 2000+ | Very high | Finer grind, longer pre-infusion | +1-2°C from roast baseline |

### Key Varieties

- **Gesha/Geisha:** Gentle — 93-95°C, 7-8 bar, bloom, 1:2.5-1:3. High pressure destroys floral top notes.
- **SL28/SL34:** High temps (94-96°C) to unlock acidity; 8-9 bar; 1:2.5-1:3. Needs full extraction.
- **Bourbon:** 92-94°C, 9 bar, classic profiles. Forgiving and balanced.
- **Typica:** 92-94°C, 9 bar, classic. Slightly more delicate than Bourbon.
- **Caturra/Catuai:** 93-95°C, 9 bar, classic or bloom. Can handle higher extraction.
- **Modern Hybrids (Castillo, Colombia):** 92-94°C, 9 bar, classic. Reliable.

## Processing Method Patterns

- **Washed:** Clean, bright — classic profiles, standard pre-infusion (4-6s)
- **Natural:** Fruity, wine-like — bloom phase, extended pre-infusion (6-10s)
- **Honey:** Between washed and natural — moderate pre-infusion (5-7s). Yellow = cleaner, black = more fruit.
- **Anaerobic:** Intense, funky — careful temperature control, extended pre-infusion

## Photo Extraction Guide

When reading a coffee bag photo, look for:
1. **Roaster name** — Usually prominent branding
2. **Coffee name** — Often a farm, region, or creative name
3. **Origin** — Country, region, farm name
4. **Process** — May say "washed," "natural," "honey," etc.
5. **Roast date** — "Roasted on [date]" — critical for freshness
6. **Tasting notes** — Flavor descriptors
7. **Altitude** — Sometimes listed
8. **Variety** — Bourbon, Typica, Gesha, SL28, etc.

If any info is missing from the bag, search for it online.
