# Home Roasting & Espresso Blends

Building espresso blends from green coffee for milk drinks and Americanos — component roles, roast-level targets, and worked example recipes.

This guide is for home roasters who buy green beans and want repeatable espresso blends. It focuses on blends destined for **cappuccino, latte, flat white, and Americano** — drinks where the coffee is diluted by milk or water and must keep its body, sweetness, and chocolate/nut depth rather than show off bright, delicate acidity.

---

## What a Milk / Americano Blend Needs

Steamed milk and hot-water dilution both flatten a shot the same way: they mute high, floral, acidic notes and reward low, heavy, sweet ones. A blend built for these drinks wants four things:

1. **Chocolate / nutty backbone** — the flavours milk loves and the ones that survive dilution. Almost always comes from a Brazilian (or similar low-acid Central/South American) base.
2. **Body and crema** — so the shot doesn't go thin under 4–6 oz of milk or a long Americano. Comes from roast development, robusta, and naturally heavy beans.
3. **Sweetness** — caramel and milk-chocolate sugars developed through a medium-dark to dark roast. Under-developed roasts taste sour and grassy in milk.
4. **Low-to-moderate acidity** — bright, floral, fruity beans (washed Ethiopians, high-grown Kenyans) largely disappear under milk and turn sharp in an Americano. Use them sparingly or not at all.

> See `MILK_AND_DRINKS.md` for steaming technique and drink specs, and `COFFEE_PROCESSING.md` for how green-bean processing changes extraction.

## Component Roles

Think of a blend as a base plus accents, not an equal committee of beans.

| Role | Typical beans | Contributes | Watch out for |
|------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Base (50–65%)** | Brazilian natural, low-grown washed | Chocolate/nut, sweetness, crema, body. Cheap and forgiving. | None — this is the workhorse. Make it the majority. |
| **Body / caramel (20–30%)** | Colombian, Peruvian, Guatemalan SHB | Rounds out body, adds caramel and structure. | High-grown beans (SHB) are dense and want more roast development to open up. |
| **Crema / punch (≤20%)** | Robusta | Thick, persistent crema; caffeine kick; heavy body. | Keep **≤20%** or it turns rubbery/burnt-tyre. Needs a darker roast to lose its harsh edge. |
| **Depth / low-acid (10–20%)** | Aged or monsooned beans (Old Brown Java, Monsooned Malabar) | Earthy, woody, very low acidity; makes a blend taste "rounder" in milk. | Low moisture — roasts fast and unevenly. Drop earlier than the rest (see below). |
| **Accent (≤15%, optional)** | Washed Ethiopian, Central American | A lift of aroma/complexity. | Disappears under milk; only worth it in short milk drinks (cortado, piccolo). |

## Bean-by-Bean Reference

Common green origins and how each behaves in a **milk / Americano** blend. Roast targets use the level scale defined below. "Best share" is a starting suggestion for blends of this kind — not a hard limit.

| Bean (origin type) | Process & character | Role in milk/Americano blends | Best share | Roast target | Notes |
|--------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| **Brazilian (e.g. Santos)** | Natural / pulped natural. Low acid, chocolate, nutty, heavy. | **Base** — the backbone of almost any milk blend. | 50–65% | Full City+ | Cheap, forgiving, great crema and sweetness. Make it the majority. |
| **Colombian (e.g. Bucaramanga)** | Washed. Balanced, caramel, medium-full body. | **Body / sweetness** rounding out the base. | 20–30% | Full City+ | Reliable all-rounder; integrates easily. |
| **Peruvian** | Washed. Mild, soft, nutty-sweet, low acid. | **Softener** — smooths and sweetens. | 15–25% | Full City | Gentle and lower-grown; don't over-roast or you lose the sweetness. |
| **Guatemalan SHB (e.g. El Fogon)** | Washed, dense high-grown. Cocoa, structure, medium acidity. | **Body / structure**, esp. for Americanos. | 15–25% | Full City+ → early Vienna | Dense — wants extra development to open up into cocoa depth. |
| **Costa Rican SHB (e.g. Tarrazú)** | Washed, high-grown. Bright, clean, citrus, medium body. | **Accent / body** — adds clarity. | 10–20% | Full City+ | On the bright side for milk; roast a touch darker or keep the share small. |
| **Ethiopian Yirgacheffe** | Washed. Floral, citrus, tea-like, bright, light body. | **Aroma accent only.** | ≤10–15% | Full City | Largely vanishes under milk and turns sharp in an Americano — reserve for short drinks (cortado/piccolo). |
| **Ethiopian Djimma / Jimma** | Usually natural. Earthy, winey, fuller body, lower acid than Yirgacheffe. | **Depth / rustic accent** — far more milk-friendly than washed Ethiopians. | 10–20% | Full City+ | The Ethiopian to reach for in a milk blend. Natural lots can be wild — taste before committing a big share. |
| **Old Brown Java (aged)** | Aged. Earthy, woody, very low acid, heavy. | **Depth** — makes a blend taste rounder in milk. | 10–20% | Start of second crack (drop **early**) | Low moisture — roasts fast; trust the audible crack over a fixed DTR. |
| **Monsooned Malabar** | Monsooned. Low acid, woody/spicy, musty, heavy body. | **Body / depth** and crema. | 10–20% | Early second crack **only** | Low density — scorches fast and unevenly; drop noticeably lighter than the rest. |
| **Robusta** | High caffeine, heavy crema, harsh if under-roasted. | **Crema / punch** for Italian-style blends. | ≤20% | Vienna | Roast darker than everything else to shed its rubbery edge. Above 20% it dominates. |

> The four worked blends below are built mostly from the milk-friendly beans in this table. The bright, washed, high-grown lots (Yirgacheffe, Tarrazú) are deliberately minor or absent — they shine as single origins or in short milk drinks, not as a major share of a latte or Americano blend.

## Roast Levels & Development

Bean-probe **drop temperatures do not transfer between roasters** — densities, airflow, and probe placement all differ. The two things that *do* transfer are **audible cracks** and **development time ratio (DTR)**. Anchor your targets to those.

**Roast-level scale (milk/Americano sweet spot is Full City to Vienna):**

| Level | Audible marker | Approx. Agtron | Character in milk |
|-------|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **City+** | Just past end of first crack, no second crack | ~55–60 | Often too bright/sour for milk |
| **Full City** | First scattered *snaps* of second crack | ~50 | Sweet, caramel, still some clarity |
| **Full City+** | Second crack rolling, surface just sheening | ~45 | Rich, chocolatey — the milk-drink default |
| **Vienna** | Well into second crack, oily sheen developing | ~38–40 | Dark, bittersweet, big body — bold/Italian style |

**Development Time Ratio (DTR)** = time from start of first crack to drop ÷ total roast time.

- **18–20%** — keeps a lighter, sweeter blend bright-sweet rather than roasty.
- **20–22%** — the all-round default for milk blends; good body without ashiness.
- **22–24%** — for dark/Italian blends; smooths robusta and builds syrupy body.

> **DTR caveat for low-moisture beans.** Aged and monsooned beans (Old Brown Java, Monsooned Malabar) hit first crack early and develop fast because they're already dry. A fixed DTR can *over*-roast them in real terms — trust the audible second-crack marker over the percentage for these.

## Roast Components Separately, Then Blend

Always roast each component **to its own target and combine after cooling** ("post-blend"). The beans differ in density and moisture, so a single roast curve over a pre-mixed batch gives you scorched robusta next to under-developed SHB. Robusta, monsooned, and aged beans in particular behave nothing like fresh African/Central American lots.

Weigh and combine by **green weight** for the ratios below.

## Worked Example Blends

Four blends spanning the milk/Americano range, described by origin type so they translate to whatever green beans you can source. Each lists ratio (by green weight), per-component roast target, and a DTR suggestion.

### 1. Everyday Flat White — the all-rounder
**60% Brazilian natural · 25% Colombian · 15% aged Java**

The daily driver: chocolate-nut base, caramel sweetness, earthy roundness. Forgiving to dial in, thick crema, sails through milk.

- Brazilian, Colombian → **Full City+** (drop a few seconds into rolling second crack).
- Aged Java → **start of second crack** (earlier — it's low-moisture and runs away).
- **DTR 20%.**

### 2. Italian Bar — big crema, bold kick
**50% Brazilian natural · 20% Robusta · 20% Monsooned Malabar · 10% aged Java**

The classic Italian milk-bar formula: thick persistent crema, real caffeine punch, almost no acidity. Stands up to milk and to a long Americano.

- Brazilian → **Full City+ / early Vienna.**
- Robusta → solid **Vienna** (it needs the heat to lose its harsh edge).
- Monsooned Malabar → **early second crack only** — low-density, scorches fast, drop noticeably lighter than the rest.
- Aged Java → **start of second crack.**
- **DTR 22–24%.** Keep robusta ≤20% or it turns rubbery.

### 3. Smooth Caramel Latte — sweet and round
**50% Brazilian natural · 30% Colombian · 20% Peruvian**

A softer, sweeter, lower-bitterness profile leaning toward caramel and milk chocolate. Good if the Italian Bar is too intense.

- All three → **Full City** (drop right as the first snaps of second crack appear).
- Peruvian is softer/lower-grown — don't push it past first snaps or you lose its sweetness.
- **DTR 18–20%.**

### 4. Rich Americano — depth without harshness
**55% Brazilian natural · 25% Guatemalan SHB · 20% aged Java**

Built to hold up when diluted with hot water: dark cocoa, smooth finish, no sourness when long. Also works in milk.

- Brazilian, aged Java → **Full City+.**
- Guatemalan SHB → a touch past the others, into **early Vienna** (dense high-grown bean — wants the extra development to give cocoa depth on dilution).
- **DTR 20–22%.**

## Quick Reference

| Blend | Base | Body | Crema/depth | Target level | DTR |
|-------|------|------|-------------|--------------|-----|
| Everyday Flat White | 60% Brazil | 25% Colombian | 15% aged Java | Full City+ | 20% |
| Italian Bar | 50% Brazil | — | 20% robusta · 20% Malabar · 10% Java | Vienna | 22–24% |
| Smooth Caramel Latte | 50% Brazil | 30% Colombian · 20% Peruvian | — | Full City | 18–20% |
| Rich Americano | 55% Brazil | 25% Guatemalan SHB | 20% aged Java | Full City+ → Vienna | 20–22% |

**Rules of thumb:**
- Make a Brazilian (or similar low-acid) bean the **majority base** of any milk/Americano blend.
- Keep **robusta ≤20%**; roast it darker than everything else.
- **Post-blend** — roast each component to its own marker, combine after cooling.
- For aged/monsooned beans, trust the **audible second crack** over a fixed DTR.
- Bright/floral beans are for short milk drinks at most — they vanish in a latte or Americano.
